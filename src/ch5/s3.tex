\wpr{5.3.1} If $x_1, x_2, x_3 \ldots$ are a sequence of integers with $x_i|x_{i-1}$ for all $i$, then there is $n$ such that $x_i = \pm x_{i+1}$ for all $i \le n$.
\wpr{5.3.2} Let $p$ be prime and let $a_1 \ge a_2 \ge \ldots \ge a_m$ and $b_1 \ge b_2 \ge \ldots \ge b_n$ be positive integers. If $A = C_{p^{\alpha_1}} \times \cdots \times C_{p^{\alpha_m}} \cong B = C_{p^{b_1} \times \cdots \times C_{p^{b_n}}}$ then $m = n$ and $a_i = b_i$ for all $1 \le i \le m$.
